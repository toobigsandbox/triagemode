The Sculptor

"Water carves, carries, and deposits. It never stops."

The Law: Water is a liquid. It follows the path of least resistance. From the highlands (Source) to the lowlands (Sea), it changes the face of the Earth.

Simulation: Erosion Cycle

CARVE CARRY DEPOSIT

The River Path

Source → Course → Banks → Delta. The delta is where the river finally "gives up" its burden.

V vs. U

Rivers cut like a saw (V-shaped). Glaciers scrape like a heavy bulldozer (U-shaped).

Expansion Force

Ice is the only matter that expands. It breaks mountains into pieces from the inside out.

The Water Cycle

Evaporation → Condensation → Precipitation. The Earth's continuous recycling machine.

The Engine

"Invisible, but heavy. Moving, but silent."

The Law: Air has weight (Pressure). Hot air rises, Cold air sinks. This simple dance creates every wind and storm on Earth.

Simulation: Convection Cells

Pressure Play

Rising air = Low Pressure (Light). Sinking air = High Pressure (Heavy). Wind always moves from High to Low.

Coriolis Effect

The Earth spins so fast that the winds get "slanted." We cannot draw a straight line on a spinning sphere.

The Doldrums

At the equator, air goes straight up. No horizontal wind means sailors got stuck for weeks.
