The Stem

The highway that connects the thirsty roots to the hungry leaves.

The Mission

The stem has two vital jobs: Support (holding the leaves up to the sun so they can eat) and Transport (carrying water up from the roots and food down from the leaves).

The Hook

"Plants eat with their heads and drink with their feet."

The stem is the straw. We prove it by putting celery in red water. The red dye races up the Xylem tubes against gravity!

Adaptation

Dr. Montessori noticed that plants, like humans, solve problems. Some stems are "Lazy" (Procumbent) and lay on the ground (Strawberry). Others are "Clever" (Climbing) and use hooks or tendrils to grab the sun without building a trunk!
